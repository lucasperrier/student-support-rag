# Internships and Work-Study (Sample Document)

> Synthetic demo document for the RAG pipeline. Not an official ESILV document.

Internships are a core part of the ESILV curriculum. Each year of the engineering cycle
includes a mandatory work experience.

**Internship duration.** First-year engineering students complete a one- to two-month
discovery internship. Second-year students complete a three- to four-month assistant
engineer internship. Final-year students complete a four- to six-month end-of-studies
internship that often leads to a first job.

**Internship agreement.** Before any internship starts, the student, the host company,
and the school must sign an internship agreement (*convention de stage*). The agreement
specifies the dates, the supervisor, the assignment, and the gratuity. A signed
agreement is mandatory; no student may start an internship without one.

**Gratuity.** In France, internships longer than two months must be paid a minimum
monthly gratuity set by law. The exact amount is reviewed every year.

**Work-study (alternance).** From the second year, students may choose an
apprenticeship contract that alternates periods at the school and in a company. The
company covers the tuition fees and pays a salary. A change of company tutor must be
declared to the school's work-study office.
